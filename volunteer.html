Who You'll Work With

Placements built around the many communities of East Africa

Our programs span far more than one culture. Around Arusha you'll meet Waarusha farmers and Maasai pastoralists; near Kilimanjaro, Chagga coffee-growing communities; on the coast, Swahili fishing towns; and in and around the region's cities, Kikuyu, Luo, Kalenjin and many other neighbors. You'll work alongside local staff — not just visit them — on projects the community itself has asked for.

Home-stays are optional but popular: many volunteers spend at least part of their placement living with a local family — of whichever community hosts that placement — sharing meals, daily chores, and evening conversation in Swahili.

Common Questions

Volunteer FAQ

Do I need to speak Swahili or a local language?

No. Orientation includes basic Swahili for every volunteer, plus a few essential phrases in the local language of your specific placement — whether that's Maa, Chagga, Luo or another. Most coordinators and host families speak conversational English.

Will I stay with a local family?

It depends on the program and your preference. Many placements offer optional home-stays with local host families — Maasai, Chagga, Swahili or otherwise, depending on the community that hosts the project; others use a shared volunteer house. This is confirmed before you book.

What's included in the program fee?

Airport pickup, accommodation, most meals, in-country orientation, 24/7 emergency support and project materials. Flights, visas and personal spending are not included.

Can I combine volunteering with a safari?

Yes — most volunteers add a Serengeti, Ngorongoro or cultural village safari at the end of their placement. Our team can book this alongside your application.
